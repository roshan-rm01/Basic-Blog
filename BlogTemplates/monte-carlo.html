Monte Carlo Simulation

Programmed using Python

A Monte Carlo Simulation is a probability simulation used to model different outcomes. Essentially it's a model to predict the different outcomes of a specific scenario. The simulation can be used in a variety of fields such as finance and science.

In this case I developed a Monte Carlo simulation to model the pricing of a call option. A call option is a contract that gives an individual the right but not obligation to buy an asset (financial security) at a pre-determined price. For example an individual can buy a call option on Amazon stock when the price is at $3000 so in the future if the stock rises to $3500, the individual can buy the Amazon stock when it was $3000, thus making $500 in profit. If the stock plummets to $2500, the individual does not need to buy the Amazon stock as they are not obliged to do so.

For the project, I used the Python libraries Matplotlib, Numpy and Pandas. The finance behind this model is quite complex to go into detail but in general the model is based on the Black Scholes Model which is a mathematical equation. The libraries Matplotlib and Pandas were used to extract data from an Excel spreadsheet and plot all the possible outcomes of the call option price onto a graph. Numpy was used to perform mathematical calculations. The simulation runs a certain number of times in order to get a convergence of values. Once there is a convergence of values then that value should be best used as the price of a call option.
